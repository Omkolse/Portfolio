# Differential Gear Robotic Joint Module

## Overview

The Differential Gear Robotic Joint Module is a compact 2-DoF actuation system designed for robotic arms and modular robotics platforms. The system uses a differential servo configuration to generate coordinated dual-axis motion from two high-torque servos, making it suitable for applications such as robotic wrist joints, shoulder joints, and precision manipulators.

The entire mechanism is fully 3D printable, allowing rapid prototyping and easy integration into custom robotics projects.

## Key Features

• **Differential Motion System**
Two servos operate in a differential configuration, enabling two independent axes of motion through coordinated servo control.

• **Compact 2-DoF Mechanism**
The design provides dual-axis rotation in a small form factor, ideal for robotic wrists, shoulders, or articulated joints.

• **Low-Backlash Belt Drive**
Motion is transmitted using a GT2 timing belt (6 mm width, 2 mm pitch) to ensure smooth power transfer with minimal backlash.

• **High Torque Actuation**
The module uses TowerPro MG996R high-torque servo motors, providing sufficient torque for robotic arm movement and load handling.

• **Fully 3D Printable Structure**
All structural components are designed for FDM 3D printing, enabling easy manufacturing and quick design iteration.

## Mechanical Design

The joint module is engineered for stability, precision, and modular integration.

Key mechanical aspects include:

* 8×22×7 mm ball bearings for stable and low-friction rotation
* Reinforced gear housing for handling servo torque
* Optimized belt path for smooth motion transmission
* Compact layout to minimize overall joint size

The design ensures precise rotation while maintaining structural strength under load.

## Hardware Components

**Actuators**
2 × TowerPro MG996R High-Torque Servo Motors

**Transmission System**
GT2 Timing Belt
Width: 6 mm
Pitch: 2 mm

**Bearings**
8 × 22 × 7 mm Ball Bearings

**Fasteners**
Standard M3 bolts for assembly
